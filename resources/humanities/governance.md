## Overview

Public accountability is the expectation that institutions explain decisions, manage resources responsibly, and allow citizens to scrutinize public work. Strong governance depends on transparency, participation, reliable records, and enforceable standards.

## Accountability Mechanisms

| Mechanism | Role |
|-----------|------|
| Open records | Makes decisions and spending reviewable |
| Public consultation | Allows affected communities to contribute |
| Independent audits | Checks whether resources were used properly |
| Clear procurement rules | Reduces favoritism and waste |
| Complaint channels | Gives residents a way to report problems |

## Connection to Open Knowledge

Open libraries, transparent documentation, and public issue tracking can make government knowledge systems easier to inspect, improve, and trust.
